Chim Lạc Motif

The totemic bird of the Lạc Việt people, often depicted flying counterclockwise across the face of the bronze drum. A symbol of ethnic origin.

Bronze Drum Motif

A symbol of spiritual power. At its center is the sacred Sun star, while the concentric circles reflect the cosmology of wet-rice civilization.

Giao Long Motif

A mythical creature ruling the waters of ancient Vietnamese imagination, Giao Long is closely tied to the custom of tattooing the body to ward off aquatic beasts, carrying meanings of protection and primal strength.

Đại Việt Sử Ký Toàn Thư records that in the earliest days, our people were often harmed by Giao Long when entering the water, so Hùng Vương taught them to tattoo images of aquatic creatures on their bodies so that these beings would no longer attack them. This legend is reinterpreted into low-density embroidered motifs that evoke the lines of ancient tattoo markings, placed discreetly in hidden areas such as the inner flap of the garment or inside the pockets of the cargo pants.

Sidewalk Culture

Inherently Vietnamese

Chapter IV does not borrow from royal court ornament. Here, heritage is "everyday popular culture". It celebrates roomy oversized and genderless silhouettes, maximizing comfort in order to recook heritage into "Street-Heritage".
